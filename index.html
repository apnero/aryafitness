Aerial Classes

Monday

  • 5:10pm Pole Control - Level 3 - Allison

    Pole is a journey of ones strength and perseverance. In this class you feel the excitement of inverting and ambition to develop personal pole goals. Remember bruises are badges of honor and a step closer to achieving your goals!

    Pole Control is designed to incorporate the strength and fluidity learned in Pole Flow and Pole High to comfortably begin the intermediate levels of inverting, variations on climbing, shoulder mounts and more intricate spins/tricks. This class gives the student the foundation of body control, confidence with the concept of inverting, and builds the center core strength necessary. Different variations and adjuncts of climb will in introduced and developed based upon skill level.

    Basic handstands/dismounts from inversions will be explored as well as spins that may require more technique and strength. Spin pole will be used.

  • 6:10pm Spinner - Level 2 - Alex

    Learn how to control spin speeds and gain grace and fluidity. Practice flow and integrate different floor tricks together into spinner combos.

  • 7:10pm Pole Flow - Level 1 - Allison

    The beginning stage of pole fitness. Your feet are still on the ground or hovering. You will learn the mechanics: different grips, walking around the pole, proximity to the pole, weight distribution, momentum and speed control. Our goal is to get you comfortable around the pole while conditioning your muscles to be able to advance. You will learn the basic spins and climbing. Pole Flow is a minimum of 8 classes series, after which members will be assessed by the instructor and either advance to the next level (Pole High - Level 2) or continue in the current level. We want members to progress when they are ready.

  • 7:10pm Beginner Hoop - Alex

    If you have never taken an aerial hoop (Lyra) class and are new to aerial apparatus, this is the class for you. In this class you will learn the various techniques to enter and exit from the hoop along with conditioning, strength and flexibility exercises.

    Aerial hoop (aka lyra) is a circular apparatus suspended from a single point. Build strength, showcase flexibility and learn to flip, turn, roll, pose, and transition between positions. Classes begin with basic skills and conditioning and gradually progress into fluid choreographed sequences.

  • 8:10pm Dynamics of Aerial Hammock - Alex

    In this class you will use aerial hammocks to achieve weightlessness, spinal decompression, gentle stretching, and core stabilization. Allow the sensation of flight to bring playfulness and freedom to your fitness class. Decompress and elongate your spine through weightless inversions. The hammock supports your body’s contours, allowing you to go deeper into stretches and traditional yoga poses. Finally, let the gentle swinging of the hammock relax your mind and release your daily stress.

    Hammock, also called Sling, is one piece of fabric with the two tails connected to the ceiling at the same point to create a trapeze-like apparatus. In this class you will learn skills to stand on the loop, hang from the loop, and wrap yourself in many different positions. As you become more comfortable, you can learn drops, slides, and add spinning.

Tuesday

  • 5:10pm Pole High - Level 2 - Allison

    Moving up in the pole world! This is the next step in Arya's Pole Fitness Classes. In this class you will perfect a variety of climbs and dismounts from the pole. You will also learn the beginnings of inversions and gain different grips with your hands and thighs. Students will learn how to properly invert and dismount from an upside down position. Basic inversion tricks will also be taught and mastered before progressing to the next level.

    This class works on becoming comfortable up high on the pole as well as being upside down.

  • 6:10pm Pole Finity - Level 5 - Nicole

    Pole-finity is designed for the over achieving pole students hungry push their strength and flexibility to maximum capacity. Taking notes from the pro’s social media accounts and bringing it to life here in Brookfield, CT. Students will not be disappointed with advanced inverted combinations learned in this class.

    Pole-finity and BEYOND! Infinite numbers of trick and transitions, new tricks developing everyday!

Wednesday

  • 5:10pm Pole Powerhouse - Level 3 - Alex

    This class is not for the faint at heart, definitely a huge challenge with great rewards on a student pole progression.

    This class is designed for the hardcore pole students that are interested in building their pole stamina and strength. Pole Powerhouse is constructed from a bootcamp perspective of pole. It is required that the student is completely comfortable inverting on their own. Training will include consecutive inversions and pole climbs. Students will benefit greatly from the strength and consistency that will be developed in this class

  • 6:10pm Polesque - Level 2 - Mary Beth

    Polesque is a routine based class designated to one specific song which includes a video produced at the end of the session. This class is done in either heels or bare feet depending on the dynamic of the song selected. The routine includes a handspring combination, dance sequence, floor work, and spin/static pole combination. Routines are usually within 3-4 minutes. The hard work and development of the routine is a rewarding experience for the student. A new routine is produced every 8 weeks.

  • 7:10pm Advanced Hoop - Alex

    Advanced Hoop classes are for those students who have demonstrated proficiency with our level 1 class on the apparatus. These classes focus on more complex skills, fluid transitions, and short sequences. You’ll spend more time up in the air building endurance and stamina, as well as strength. If interested, Level 2 students with instructor permission may begin building their own choreography and join our choreography classes and student showcases.

LATE POLICY:

PLEASE DO NOT BE LATE.

IF YOU ARE MORE THAN 10 MIN LATE TO CLASS, WE RESERVE THE RIGHT TO GIVE YOUR SPOT TO ANOTHER STUDENT ON THE WAITING LIST. YOU WILL NOT BE ALLOWED TO JOIN THE CLASS AND WILL NOT BE REFUNDED YOUR MONEY.

You should be dressed and ready to start your session at the scheduled time. We suggest that you arrive to the studio at least 15 minutes before your scheduled class to avoid late cancellation charges. This is a safety issue, and we ask that you respect both our instructors and other students by showing up on time.

PAYMENT:

We use Mind Body Online for scheduling all classes. You will have to set up an account with mindbody in order to schedule classes. This allows you to reserve a spot for your chosen class.  You can pay for your class at the time you register using Mindbody. We take cash, checks, and credit cards at the studio.

LIABILITY WAIVER:

When you sign up for a session you read and agree to our terms and conditions. All members must have a signed liability waiver on file.  If you are under the age of 18, we require a parental consent. Your parent needs to come to the studio with you, so they can sign the release form with a copy of their ID. Prior to participation in any of Arya Fitness Studio’s activities, you must have read and agreed to our terms and conditions. If you have not signed a waiver you cannot participate.

CANCELLATION POLICY:

At Arya Fitness Studio we have a 12 hour cancellation policy. As long as you cancel at least 12 hours before your scheduled class or wait list, you will not be charged for that class. If you late cancel, you will be charged for that class and will not be refunded your money.

WORKSHOPS: Workshops are non-refundable. If you purchase a workshop and cannot attend you may transfer your workshop to someone else. Please contact us with any questions.

PRIVATE CLASSES: We ask that you let us know at least 24 hours ahead of time if you need to cancel a private class, otherwise you will lose your payment.

There are no refunds on late cancellations.

Arya Fitness Studio reserves the right to cancel any session of classes that does not have 2 or more members registered. Members who are registered in a class that gets canceled will be allowed to transfer into a different class or receive a refund.

REFUNDS:

Workshops and memberships are non-refundable. Once you purchase your classes, make sure you know the expiration date and use all classes before that date. We will not extend the expiration date. Class packs expire as follows: 10 pack in 3 months and 20 pack in 6 months.

DROP IN CLASSES:

While some drop in classes are available, we encourage you to sign up for a session. This helps you commit to a routine that will help you progress with more ease, and guess what – it saves you money too! Drop ins will cost you a few extra dollars. It also helps us to offer a bigger variety of classes as class spots fill up. Speaking of spots, we maintain limited class sizes to assure each student is getting proper instruction and pre-registration guarantees a spot. If you do decide to drop in, you will be asked to sign in and pay before class. You will also need to have signed a waiver to participate.

AGE LIMITATIONS:

If you are under the age of 18, we require a parental consent. Your parent needs to come to the studio with you, so they can sign the release form with a copy of their ID. This is for insurance liability purposes. Feel free to sign up for class, but make sure you are accompanied by your parent when coming in for your first class.

SKILL REQUIREMENTS:

Arya Fitness Studio offers many classes for beginners that have no prerequisites. In these classes instructors will work with you to help you attain your own personal goals. You will never be asked to perform a set of skills that is unsafe for you or that you are not ready for, but we will help you to reach past your former personal best. Some intermediate and advanced classes have specific prerequisites (i.e. other classes that we offer, shown mastery of a set of skills, or studio owner’s and instructor’s approval) that are in place to assure everyone’s safety. Check out our class description for prerequisites and details or ask Daniela or Allison if unsure if a certain class is right for you.

ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGES:

No alcohol is allowed to be consumed before any physical activities at the studio. If you are intoxicated when arriving to our studio, you will not be allowed to participate in class and will not be refunded your money.

PETS:

Animals are not allowed at the studio.

FOOD & BEVERAGES:

Food & Beverages with the exception of bottled water, are not allowed in the studio area. You may use the waiting area to eat or drink anything else.

PROPER ATTIRE:

If you are taking an aerial hoop or hammock class you will want to wear fitted pants that go to your mid-calf or longer, as your knees will be wrapping around the apparatuses. Wear a shirt that can be tucked in or is fitted for upside down moves. Leotards and dance tights will also work. Everyone will be barefoot, dance shoes or socks are allowed as well. We also ask that you don’t wear anything that has buttons, buckles, snaps, grommets, zippers, studs, or anything that can snag our equipment, this includes jewelry (rings, large or dangly body jewelry ,etc.). Long hair should be tied up to avoid being tangled in the equipment. Do NOT wear any kind of lotion, cream, or oil of any sort on any part of your body the day of or the night before class. Chemicals and oils in these lotions damage and ruin the equipment and cause it to be unsafe to use. Please cover all cuts, scrapes or any open wounds with a band-aid and with athletic tape before class time to prevent the wound from reopening and getting blood on any equipment. Athletic tape is required as a band-aid will come right off after a few minutes of climbing on the equipment. We ask this for your own safety and that of our equipment.  Thank you for respecting this.

SAFETY:

If you, or another student are injured in class, please let an instructor know immediately.

Be sure to respect your body. If you are having a day when you feel off, let your instructor know and stay close to the ground. Instructors will request this of you if they feel you are not in an aerial frame of mind that day (including such things as sleep deprivation, not having eaten enough, illness, etc.). It’s nothing personal, we all have off days when we should not be in the air.

There can be no drug or alcohol use prior to coming to class. This includes medicines which diminishes judgement or motor skills. Please use good sense, but also know that if an instructor suspects that anyone is impaired, the instructor will prevent that person from participating, practicing, using equipment, and may ask them to leave the studio.

BEHAVIOR AT THE STUDIO:

Please respect your instructors and fellow students and leave your phone conversations outside the studio while in class. This goes to text messaging as well.

No street shoes in the studio.

We do not tolerate bullying or disrespecting of other students and their personal space. Such behavior will result of removal from the studio.

At Arya Fitness Studio we want to maintain a welcoming environment for people of all ages, levels, and abilities. Abuse of any kind will not be tolerated. Please welcome newer students, showing them that Arya has a playful and inspiring atmosphere, which encourages health and community.

Arya Fitness Studio is a place for everyone to learn at their own pace, and to provide an atmosphere of encouragement and positivity! Please help us keep Arya awesome by treating each other with respect.

SPACE RENTALS:

Space rentals are for personal use only and are not for profit purposes: Practice, rehearsals, videotaping, private parties, etc.

24 hour cancellation policy when booking a private space rental, you will not be refunded your money with less than 24 hours cancellation.

Equipment Rental is an additional cost of $15 per apparatus.

Space rental are by the hour, we do not do half hour rentals.
