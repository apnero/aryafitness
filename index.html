Aerial Classes

Monday

  • 4:10pm Pole Flow - Level 1 - Allison

    Explanation of the different grips, pole walk, and making students understand that if pole was easy everyone would be doing it. It is a GREAT challenge and if your up for it you will be rewarded mind and body.

    This is class is designed for the beginner pole student to learn the fundamentals of pole tricks, transitions, grips, and overall confidence with pole dancing. Students will be required to do tricks/spins on their right and left sides, unless an injury prevents them from doing so. The pole climb will be introduced and expanded apon on an individual basis. It is a extremely important to have strong foundation to build on and that is exactly what the student will achieve in this class. Spin pole will be introduced.

  • 5:10pm Pole Control - Level 2 - Allison

  • 6:10pm Spinner - Level 2 - Alex

    Learn how to control spin speeds and gain grace and fluidity. Practice flow and integrate different floor tricks together into spinner combos.

  • 7:10pm Beginner Hoop - Alex

    If you have never taken an aerial hoop (Lyra) class and are new to aerial apparatus, this is the class for you. In this class you will learn the various techniques to enter and exit from the hoop along with conditioning, strength and flexibility exercises.

    Aerial hoop (aka lyra) is a circular apparatus suspended from a single point. Build strength, showcase flexibility and learn to flip, turn, roll, pose, and transition between positions. Classes begin with basic skills and conditioning and gradually progress into fluid choreographed sequences.

Tuesday

  • 5:10pm Open Pole - Allison

    Open pole is a make up class for anyone who wasn't able to make it to their usual class and would like to practice. It's not a structured class but an instructor will be there to guide and help you with any previously taught moves.

  • 6:10pm Pole finity - Level 4 - Nicole

    Pole-finity is designed for the over achieving pole students hungry push their strength and flexibility to maximum capacity. Taking notes from the pro’s social media accounts and bringing it to life here in Brookfield, CT. Students will not be disappointed with advanced inverted combinations learned in this class.

    Pole-finity and BEYOND! Infinite numbers of trick and transitions, new tricks developing everyday!

  • 7:10pm Pole Control - Level 2 - Nicole

    Pole is a journey of ones strength and perseverance. In this class you feel the excitement of inverting and ambition to develop personal pole goals. Remember bruises are badges of honor and a step closer to achieving your goals! Pole Control is designed to incorporate the strength and fluidity learned in Pole Flow to comfortably begin the basics of inverting, variations on climbing, and more intricate spins/tricks. This class gives the student the foundation of body control, confidence with the concept of inverting, and builds the center core strength necessary. Different variations and adjuncts of climb will in introduced and developed based upon skill level. Basic handstands/dismounts from inversions will be explored as well as spins that may require more technique and strength. Spin pole will be used.

Wednesday

  • 5:10pm Pole Powerhouse - Level 2 - Alex

    This class is not for the faint at heart, definitely a huge challenge with great rewards on a student pole progression.

    This class is designed for the hardcore pole students that are interested in building their pole stamina and strength. Pole Powerhouse is constructed from a bootcamp perspective of pole. It is required that the student is completely comfortable inverting on their own. Training will include consecutive inversions and pole climbs. Students will benefit greatly from the strength and consistency that will be developed in this class.

  • 6:10pm Polesque - Level 2 - Mary Beth

    Polesque is a routine based class designated to one specific song which includes a video produced at the end of the session. This class is done in either heels or bare feet depending on the dynamic of the song selected. The routine includes a handspring combination, dance sequence, floor work, and spin/static pole combination. Routines are usually within 3-4minutes. The hard work and development of the routine is a rewarding experience for the student. A new routine is produced every 8 weeks.

  • 7:10pm Advanced Hoop - Alex

    Advanced Hoop classes are for those students who have demonstrated proficiency with our level 1 class on the apparatus. These classes focus on more complex skills, fluid transitions, and short sequences. You’ll spend more time up in the air building endurance and stamina, as well as strength. If interested, Level 2 students with instructor permission may begin building their own choreography and join our choreography classes and student showcases.

Thursday

  • 4:10pm Sensual Pole Flow - Level 1 - Allison

  • 5:10pm Sexy flexy - Level 1 - Christine

  • 6:10pm Pole Addict - Level 3 - Christine

    The name speaks for itself! Pole students who make it this far are definitely addicted! There is no going back now!

    Pole addict is designed for the students that have mastered the basics of inversions; ready to explore combinations, transitions, and intermediate inversions. With this class the student will expand their catalogue of inversions and begin understanding the concepts of transitioning from one trick another smoothly. It is required the student is comfortable with basic inversions and ready to take it to the next level of inverted combinations. Spin pole will be used.

Saturday

  • 8:10am Pole Strength - Level 1 - Nicole

  • 9:10am Pole Fluidity - Level 2 - Nicole

Fitness Classes

Monday

  • 5:10pm Aerial Stretch - Daniela

    Basics: Take your workout to the circus with Aerial Hammock Yoga. Explore this hot fitness trend and learn a few basic moves that will stretch and challenge your mind and body.

    So what is aerial stretch? Basically, you do a series of yoga type poses and stretches while suspended several inches off the ground using a long silk hammock. By counteracting gravity, aerial stretch: helps you maintain proper posture increases flexibility by relieving muscle and bone tension prevents back strain.
